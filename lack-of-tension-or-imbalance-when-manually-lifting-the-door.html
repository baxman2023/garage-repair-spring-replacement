Mention of common types of springs used in garage doors, such as torsion springs and extension springs, with a brief explanation of their functions


When it comes to garage doors, springs play a vital role in ensuring smooth operation. Two common types of springs used are torsion springs and extension springs. Let's delve into their functions!

Torsion springs are the primary type of spring found in modern garage doors. They work by twisting or rotating, storing energy as they do so. When you manually lift the door, these springs unwind and release their stored energy to help counterbalance the weight of the door. This is what allows you to lift it with ease (oh no!).

On the other hand, extension springs are typically found on older garage doors. These springs stretch or extend as the door is lifted manually. They work by creating tension that helps support the weight of the door when it's open. Without them, lifting the door would require Herculean strength (no way!).

However, sometimes problems may arise due to a lack of tension or imbalance in these springs (by any chance!). If you notice that your garage door feels unusually heavy or difficult to lift manually, this could indicate an issue with one or both of these types of springs (uh-oh!).
