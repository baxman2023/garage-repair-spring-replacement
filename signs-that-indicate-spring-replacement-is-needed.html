Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my garage door spring needs to be replaced?
Look for signs such as difficulty in opening or closing the door, sagging or uneven movement, loud noises during operation, and visible signs of wear or damage on the springs.
Can I visually inspect the garage door springs to determine if they need replacement?
Yes, you can inspect them for any visible signs of wear, rust, or damage. However, it is recommended to consult a professional for a thorough evaluation.
What are some common symptoms indicating that a garage door spring replacement is necessary?
Common symptoms include sudden inability to open the garage door entirely, significant resistance when manually operating the door, gaps between coils in extension springs, or broken pieces in torsion springs.
Are there any safety concerns associated with worn-out or damaged garage door springs?
Yes, worn-out or damaged springs can pose serious safety risks. They may break unexpectedly and cause the heavy garage door to fall abruptly. It is crucial to address any issues promptly.
Should I attempt to replace the garage door spring myself?
Garage door spring replacement can be dangerous due to the high tension involved. It is strongly advised to hire a professional technician who has expertise and proper tools to safely replace the springs.
