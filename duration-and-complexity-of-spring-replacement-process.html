Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it typically take to replace a garage door spring?
The duration of replacing a garage door spring can vary depending on various factors such as the type of spring, experience of the technician, and complexity of the job. On average, it can take around 1 to 2 hours to complete the replacement process.
What factors can affect the complexity of a garage door spring replacement?
Several factors contribute to the complexity of a garage door spring replacement, including the type (torsion or extension) and size of the spring, accessibility to the spring mechanism, any additional repairs needed for other components, and potential safety concerns.
Is it possible for me to replace a garage door spring myself?
While some individuals with advanced knowledge and experience in working with garage doors may be able to replace a spring themselves, it is generally recommended to hire a professional technician. Garage door springs are under high tension and improper handling or installation can lead to serious injuries or further damage. It is crucial to prioritize safety by relying on trained professionals for this task.
