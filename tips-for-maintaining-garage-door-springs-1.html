Frequently Asked Questions

What are the signs that indicate a need for garage door spring replacement?
Signs include difficulty in opening or closing the garage door, loud banging noises during operation, and visible gaps or breaks in the springs.
How often should garage door springs be replaced?
On average, garage door springs have a lifespan of about 10,000 cycles. Depending on usage, they may need replacement every 7-12 years.
Can I replace only one garage door spring if it breaks?
It is highly recommended to replace both springs at the same time to maintain balance and prevent uneven wear. If one spring has broken, chances are the other is nearing its end as well.
Is it safe to replace garage door springs by myself?
Garage door spring replacement can be extremely dangerous and should only be done by trained professionals. The high tension of these springs poses significant risks of injury or property damage if mishandled.
How can I extend the lifespan of my garage door springs?
Regular maintenance such as lubricating moving parts, inspecting for wear and tear, and avoiding excessive strain on the door can help prolong the life of your garage door springs.
