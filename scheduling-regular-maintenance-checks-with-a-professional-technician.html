Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule regular maintenance checks for my garage door springs?
It is recommended to schedule maintenance checks for your garage door springs at least once a year.
What are the signs that indicate I need to replace my garage door springs?
Signs of worn-out or damaged garage door springs include difficulty in opening or closing the door, excessive noise during operation, uneven movement of the door, and visible sagging or stretching of the springs.
How long do garage door springs typically last before needing replacement?
The lifespan of garage door springs varies depending on usage and quality, but they generally last around 7-9 years.
Can I perform maintenance checks on my own or should I hire a professional technician?
It is highly recommended to hire a professional technician for maintenance checks as they have the necessary expertise and tools to ensure thorough inspection and proper functioning of your garage door springs.
What are the potential risks associated with attempting to replace garage door springs myself?
DIY replacement of garage door springs can be extremely dangerous due to their high tension. Mishandling or incorrect installation can lead to serious injuries or damage. Therefore, it is best to leave this task to trained professionals.
