Frequently Asked Questions

Why is regular inspection and lubrication important for garage door spring replacement?
Regular inspection and lubrication help ensure the smooth operation of the garage door, prevent excessive wear on the springs, and extend their lifespan.
How frequently should I inspect and lubricate my garage door springs?
It is recommended to inspect and lubricate garage door springs every six months or at least once a year.
What are the signs that indicate my garage door springs need inspection or lubrication?
Signs include squeaking or grinding noises during operation, uneven movement of the door, visible rust or corrosion on the springs, or if it has been more than a year since their last inspection.
How should I go about inspecting and lubricating my garage door springs?
To inspect, visually examine the condition of the springs for any signs of damage, rust, or excessive wear. To lubricate them, use a silicone-based spray or white lithium grease to coat all moving parts of the spring system while avoiding excessive application.
