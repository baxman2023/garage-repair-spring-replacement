Frequently Asked Questions

Why does a garage door struggle to open or close smoothly when its springs need replacement?
When garage door springs start to wear out or break, it can cause an imbalance in the weight distribution. This leads to difficulties in opening and closing as the opener motor has to work harder.
What causes loud squeaking or grinding noises during garage door operation indicating spring replacement is necessary?
The noise could be due to worn-out or damaged springs. As they lose their tension and become less effective in supporting the weight of the door, they may produce friction against other components while opening or closing.
How do visible gaps, breaks, or fraying in garage door springs signify their need for replacement?
Any visible damage on the springs such as gaps between coils, breaks along their length, or frayed sections indicates that they have weakened over time and may snap soon. Its crucial to replace them before complete failure occurs.
Why should rusted or corroded springs be replaced promptly on a garage door?
Rust and corrosion weaken the structural integrity of a spring. When exposed to moisture and elements over time, it can cause metal degradation leading to potential breakage. Replacing rusty or corroded springs ensures optimal functionality and prevents accidents.
What does sudden imbalance in a garage door suggest about its springs needing replacement?
If one side of your garage door hangs lower than usual when opened halfway, it indicates an issue with the springs. Imbalance typically occurs when one spring is damaged or broken, making it necessary to replace both springs for proper functioning.
